

ARGUMENT

Count I: Deliberate Indifference to Sexual Harassment under Title IX (20 U.S.C. § 1681)

Plaintiff David Mullins brings this claim against Defendant Duquesne University of the Holy Spirit, alleging deliberate indifference to known sexual harassment in violation of Title IX. The evidence demonstrates that Duquesne University had actual knowledge of the harassment, responded in a clearly unreasonable manner, and failed to provide supportive measures, resulting in a denial of equal educational access.

Actual Knowledge

Duquesne University had actual knowledge of the harassment through various reports and communications from Plaintiff Mullins. On June 30, 2025, Mullins reported the harassment to Dean Adam Wasilko, describing a physical pursuit and confrontation by his roommate, Respondent (Exhibit B-0). Mullins also sent a formal Title IX escalation to Alicia Simpson, the Title IX Coordinator, on July 24, 2025, citing 34 C.F.R. § 106.44(a) and demanding supportive measures within 48 hours (Exhibit B-1). These reports demonstrate that Duquesne University had actual knowledge of the harassment, satisfying the first element of a Title IX claim.

Clearly Unreasonable Response (Deliberate Indifference)

Duquesne University's response to the harassment was clearly unreasonable, demonstrating deliberate indifference. Despite Mullins' repeated requests for supportive measures, the University failed to provide any meaningful assistance. On August 13, 2025, Simpson responded to Mullins' Emergency Removal Request, acknowledging that his concerns warranted further review, but ultimately disclaiming jurisdiction over the complaint on August 14, 2025 (Exhibits B-5 and B-6). This reversal, without any new factual information, demonstrates a clearly unreasonable response. As the Third Circuit held in Hall v. Millersville University, a failure to forward sexual misconduct reports to the Title IX Coordinator can constitute a clearly unreasonable response.

Severe, Pervasive, Objectively Offensive Harassment

The harassment experienced by Mullins was severe, pervasive, and objectively offensive. Respondent's behavior, including physical pursuit, intimidation, and retaliation, created a hostile environment that interfered with Mullins' educational opportunities (Exhibit D). The harassment was also objectively offensive, as evidenced by the medical documentation from Treating Physician, which confirms that Mullins' fear of Respondent was reasonable and that sharing a living space with her would cause him significant stress and worsen his medical condition (Exhibit F).

Denial of Equal Educational Access

Duquesne University's failure to provide supportive measures resulted in a denial of equal educational access for Mullins. Despite his requests for housing assistance, the University denied him access to available housing resources, forcing him to endure 90+ days of homelessness (Exhibit A). The University's failure to provide a safe and supportive environment also prevented Mullins from fully participating in his educational program, as evidenced by the academic expert declarations from External Faculty Witness (Exhibit C).

In conclusion, the evidence demonstrates that Duquesne University had actual knowledge of the harassment, responded in a clearly unreasonable manner, and failed to provide supportive measures, resulting in a denial of equal educational access. As the Supreme Court held in Davis v. Monroe County Board of Education, a funding recipient is liable for student-on-student harassment where it acts with deliberate indifference to known harassment that is severe, pervasive, and objectively offensive enough to deny access to educational opportunities.

WHEREFORE, Plaintiff David Mullins respectfully requests that this Court find in his favor on Count I, deliberate indifference to sexual harassment under Title IX.
