

ARGUMENT

This case presents a clear example of deliberate indifference to known sexual harassment, in violation of Title IX. The evidence demonstrates that Duquesne University had actual knowledge of the harassment, yet responded in a clearly unreasonable manner, denying Plaintiff David Mullins equal access to educational opportunities.

First, the University had actual knowledge of the harassment. As defined in Exhibit Z-e, actual knowledge means notice of sexual harassment or allegations of sexual harassment to a recipient's Title IX Coordinator or any official with authority to institute corrective measures. Here, Plaintiff reported the harassment to Dean Wasilko, who has authority to institute corrective measures, and later to Title IX Coordinator Alicia Simpson. (Exhibit B-0; Exhibit B-1). This report triggered the University's obligation to respond promptly and not be deliberately indifferent. (Exhibit Z-b).

Second, the University's response was clearly unreasonable. As defined in Exhibit Z-b, a school is deliberately indifferent only where its response to sexual harassment is clearly unreasonable under the known circumstances. Here, the University failed to provide any supportive measures, despite Plaintiff's repeated requests, and instead conditioned such measures on Plaintiff's willingness to meet with University officials. (Exhibit B-5; Exhibit B-5A). This response was clearly unreasonable, as it ignored Plaintiff's safety concerns and failed to provide any meaningful support. (Exhibit F).

Furthermore, the University's response was motivated by a desire to avoid liability, rather than a genuine concern for Plaintiff's well-being. As evidenced by Exhibit B-6, the University suddenly reversed its jurisdictional determination, claiming that the harassment did not meet the University's definitions of prohibited conduct, after Plaintiff refused to meet with University officials and asserted his rights under Title IX. This reversal was not based on any new factual information, but rather on Plaintiff's protected activity under § 106.71. (Exhibit B-5A; Exhibit B-6).

The University's deliberate indifference to the harassment had a profound impact on Plaintiff's educational experience. As a result of the University's failure to provide supportive measures, Plaintiff was forced to flee his home, experienced homelessness, and suffered significant emotional distress. (Exhibit F; Exhibit G). This denial of equal educational access is precisely the harm that Title IX is designed to prevent. (Davis v. Monroe County Board of Education, 526 U.S. 629, 648 (1999)).

In conclusion, the evidence demonstrates that Duquesne University had actual knowledge of the harassment, yet responded in a clearly unreasonable manner, denying Plaintiff equal access to educational opportunities. This deliberate indifference to known sexual harassment is a clear violation of Title IX. (Doe v. University of the Sciences, 961 F.3d 203, 206 (3d Cir. 2020)).

WHEREFORE, Plaintiff respectfully requests that this Court find in his favor on Count I, and grant such relief as is just and proper.
