

**ARGUMENT**

Plaintiff David Mullins brings this action against Defendant Duquesne University of the Holy Spirit, alleging deliberate indifference to sexual harassment under Title IX (20 U.S.C. § 1681). The undisputed material facts demonstrate that Duquesne University had actual knowledge of the harassment, responded in a clearly unreasonable manner, and denied Mullins equal access to educational opportunities.

**Actual Knowledge**

Duquesne University had actual knowledge of the harassment through its Title IX Coordinator, Alicia Simpson, and other officials with authority to institute corrective measures. (Exhibit B-1; Exhibit Z-e, Slide 30). On June 30, 2025, Mullins reported the harassment to Dean Adam Wasilko, who failed to take adequate action. (Exhibit B-0). Simpson acknowledged receipt of Mullins' formal Title IX complaint on July 24, 2025. (Exhibit B-1). These reports triggered Duquesne University's obligation to respond promptly and effectively.

**Clearly Unreasonable Response (Deliberate Indifference)**

Duquesne University's response to the harassment was clearly unreasonable, demonstrating deliberate indifference. (Davis v. Monroe County Board of Education, 526 U.S. 629, 648 (1999)). The university failed to provide supportive measures, such as housing assistance, despite Mullins' repeated requests. (Exhibit A; Exhibit B-0). Simpson's August 13, 2025, email offered to discuss supportive measures, but her August 14, 2025, email disclaimed jurisdiction over the complaint without providing any new factual information. (Exhibit B-5; Exhibit B-6). This reversal was unreasonable and demonstrated a lack of good faith.

**Severe, Pervasive, Objectively Offensive Harassment**

The harassment was severe, pervasive, and objectively offensive. (Exhibit D; Exhibit F). Mullins experienced physical and emotional distress, including homelessness and financial instability, due to the harassment. (Exhibit A; Exhibit F). The harassment was not isolated, as Mullins reported multiple incidents of stalking, intimidation, and coercion by his former roommate, Respondent. (Exhibit D).

**Denial of Equal Educational Access**

Duquesne University's failure to respond effectively to the harassment denied Mullins equal access to educational opportunities. (Exhibit I; Exhibit J). Mullins was forced to participate remotely in classes, despite his desire to attend in person, due to the university's failure to provide supportive measures. (Exhibit I). The university's actions, or lack thereof, created a hostile educational environment that prevented Mullins from fully participating in his graduate program.

In conclusion, the undisputed material facts demonstrate that Duquesne University had actual knowledge of the harassment, responded in a clearly unreasonable manner, and denied Mullins equal access to educational opportunities. Therefore, Plaintiff David Mullins is entitled to relief under Title IX for deliberate indifference to sexual harassment.
