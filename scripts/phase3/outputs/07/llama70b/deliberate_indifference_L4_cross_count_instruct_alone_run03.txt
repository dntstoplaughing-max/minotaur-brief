

ARGUMENT

Count I: Deliberate Indifference to Sexual Harassment under Title IX (20 U.S.C. § 1681)

Plaintiff David Mullins brings this claim against Defendant Duquesne University of the Holy Spirit, alleging deliberate indifference to known sexual harassment in violation of Title IX. To establish this claim, Plaintiff must demonstrate that the University had actual knowledge of the harassment, responded in a clearly unreasonable manner, and that the harassment was severe, pervasive, and objectively offensive, denying Plaintiff equal access to educational opportunities. (Davis v. Monroe County Board of Education, 526 U.S. 629, 648 (1999))

First, the University had actual knowledge of the harassment. On June 30, 2025, Plaintiff reported the harassment to Dean Adam Wasilko, describing a physical pursuit and confrontation by his roommate, Respondent. (Exhibit B-0) This report triggered the University's duty to respond promptly and not be deliberately indifferent. (34 C.F.R. § 106.44) The University's Title IX Coordinator, Alicia Simpson, also acknowledged receipt of the report and offered to discuss supportive measures. (Exhibit B-5)

However, the University's response was clearly unreasonable. Despite Plaintiff's repeated requests for supportive measures, including emergency housing assistance, the University failed to provide any meaningful assistance. (Exhibits B-0, B-5A, B-6) In fact, the University's response was so inadequate that it can be characterized as deliberate indifference. (Davis, 526 U.S. at 648) The University's failure to provide supportive measures, such as a no-contact order or registrar separation, allowed the harassment to continue, denying Plaintiff equal access to educational opportunities. (Exhibits B-5A, B-6)

Furthermore, the harassment was severe, pervasive, and objectively offensive. Plaintiff's roommate, Respondent, had a history of bullying and coercive behavior, including locking Plaintiff out of their apartment and pursuing him to his bedroom. (Exhibit D) This behavior created a hostile environment that interfered with Plaintiff's ability to participate in his educational program. (Exhibit R)

The University's deliberate indifference to this harassment is evident in its failure to take prompt and effective action. Despite Plaintiff's repeated reports of harassment, the University failed to provide any meaningful assistance, instead forcing Plaintiff to seek help from outside sources, including the Office for Civil Rights. (Exhibit B-3A) This failure to respond to known harassment constitutes deliberate indifference, in violation of Title IX. (Davis, 526 U.S. at 648)

In addition, the University's actions demonstrate a pattern of deliberate indifference. The University's Title IX Coordinator, Alicia Simpson, initially acknowledged Plaintiff's report and offered to discuss supportive measures, but later disclaimed jurisdiction over the complaint without explanation. (Exhibits B-5, B-6) This reversal, combined with the University's failure to provide any meaningful assistance, demonstrates a clear disregard for Plaintiff's rights under Title IX.

In conclusion, the evidence demonstrates that the University had actual knowledge of the harassment, responded in a clearly unreasonable manner, and that the harassment was severe, pervasive, and objectively offensive, denying Plaintiff equal access to educational opportunities. Therefore, Plaintiff is entitled to relief under Title IX for the University's deliberate indifference to known sexual harassment.

WHEREFORE, Plaintiff demands judgment against Defendant Duquesne University of the Holy Spirit for deliberate indifference to known sexual harassment in violation of Title IX, and requests such other and further relief as the Court deems just and proper.
