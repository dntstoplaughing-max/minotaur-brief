

ARGUMENT

Count II of the Complaint alleges that Duquesne University retaliated against Plaintiff David Mullins in violation of Title IX (20 U.S.C. § 1681). To establish a prima facie case of retaliation, Plaintiff must show: (1) protected activity, (2) an adverse action that would deter a reasonable person from engaging in the protected activity, and (3) a causal connection between the protected activity and the adverse action. (See Doe v. Mercy Catholic Medical Center, No. 16-1247 (3d Cir. 2017) (applying Title VII framework to Title IX retaliation claims))

Here, Plaintiff has engaged in continuous protected activity from June 30, 2025, through the present, including reporting sex-based harassment to Dean Wasilko, filing a formal Title IX complaint, and filing a federal lawsuit. (Exhibits B-0, B-0A, B-3A; ECF docket) This protected activity is clearly aimed at opposing sex-based harassment and seeking redress for Plaintiff's experiences. (See Kengerski v. Harper, No. 20-1307 (3d Cir. 2021) (holding that plaintiff need only "reasonably believe" that the conduct opposed violates the statute))

In response to Plaintiff's protected activity, Duquesne University has taken a series of adverse actions that would deter a reasonable person from engaging in similar activity. For example, on July 28, 2025, Dean Wasilko removed OCR from the CC line on his responsive email, despite Plaintiff's explicit request to keep OCR included. (Exhibit B-3) This action was part of a larger pattern of systematically removing OCR from the CC line on eight or more occasions, each time after Plaintiff restored OCR. (Exhibits B-3 through B-7 series) This behavior is retaliatory and would deter a reasonable person from seeking OCR's involvement in their Title IX complaint.

Additionally, on August 13, 2025, Title IX Coordinator Alicia Simpson acknowledged Title IX jurisdiction over Plaintiff's complaint, only to reverse her determination 19 hours later, without any change in the underlying facts. (Exhibits B-5, B-5A, B-5B, B-5C, B-6) This sudden and unexplained reversal of jurisdiction is a clear example of an adverse action that would deter a reasonable person from pursuing their Title IX complaint.

Furthermore, on October 6, 2025, Faculty C authorized Zoom access for Plaintiff, only to have Dr. Selcer revoke that access two days later, without any justification. (Exhibits Q, V) This revocation of Zoom access is an adverse action that would deter a reasonable person from seeking accommodations under Title IX.

The causal connection between Plaintiff's protected activity and these adverse actions is clear. For example, the removal of OCR from the CC line on Dean Wasilko's email occurred just two days after Plaintiff's OCR filing. (Exhibit B-3) Similarly, the revocation of Zoom access occurred just two days after Plaintiff testified at the TRO hearing. (Exhibits Q, V) This temporal proximity, combined with the context of the actions, establishes a clear causal connection between Plaintiff's protected activity and the adverse actions taken by Duquesne University.

In addition to these specific examples, the pattern of behavior exhibited by Duquesne University demonstrates a clear intent to retaliate against Plaintiff for his protected activity. For example, Dr. Selcer's email of February 13, 2026, fabricates "performance issues" raised by two faculty members, despite no prior mention of such issues, and threatens Plaintiff's stipend through Spring 2029. (Exhibit X-8) This email is a clear example of retaliation, and the fact that it was sent just three days after a meeting with Plaintiff, in which no "performance issues" were discussed, establishes a clear causal connection between Plaintiff's protected activity and the adverse action. (Exhibit X-6)

In conclusion, Plaintiff has established a prima facie case of retaliation under Title IX, demonstrating protected activity, adverse actions that would deter a reasonable person, and a clear causal connection between the protected activity and the adverse actions. Duquesne University's pattern of behavior, including the removal of OCR from the CC line, the reversal of Title IX jurisdiction, the revocation of Zoom access, and the fabrication of "performance issues," demonstrates a clear intent to retaliate against Plaintiff for his protected activity.

WHEREFORE, Plaintiff respectfully requests that this Court find in his favor on Count II of the Complaint and hold Duquesne University liable for retaliation under Title IX.
