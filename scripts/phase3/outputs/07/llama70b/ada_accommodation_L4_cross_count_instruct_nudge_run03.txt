

ARGUMENT

Count III: Failure to Accommodate under Section 504 (29 U.S.C. § 794) and the ADA

Plaintiff David Mullins, a doctoral candidate in the Department of Philosophy at Duquesne University, has been denied reasonable accommodations by the University, in violation of Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act and the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA). The University's failure to accommodate Plaintiff's disability has resulted in significant harm, including the loss of access to educational opportunities and emotional distress.

First, Plaintiff is a qualified individual with a disability, as required by the statute. (Exhibit F: Medical Documentation – Treating Physician, August 13 & October 1, 2025). Treating Physician, Plaintiff's treating physician, has provided documentation stating that Plaintiff does not feel safe around a specific individual on campus, and that it is reasonable for him to have this fear. (Exhibit F).

Second, Plaintiff provided notice of his disability and need for accommodation to the University. (Exhibit R: Selcer Response to Safety Concerns, July 20, 2025). In an email to Dr. Daniel Selcer, Plaintiff explained that he fears for his safety and requested assistance in navigating the University's conduct process.

Third, Plaintiff requested a reasonable accommodation, specifically a no-contact order, registrar separation, and a campus safety escort. (Exhibit I: Selcer Meeting Exchange, August 25, 2025). These accommodations are non-punitive and designed solely to preserve access, as required by the statute. (Exhibit Z-c: Supportive Measures – Mandatory, Non-Punitive, Individualized, July 28, 2020).

Fourth, the University denied Plaintiff's request for accommodation. (Exhibit I: Selcer Meeting Exchange, August 25, 2025). Dr. Selcer informed Plaintiff that the Philosophy Department could not issue the orders he requested and that he would need to follow the procedures of the University offices to seek these measures.

Fifth, the University's denial of accommodation has resulted in harm to Plaintiff. (Exhibit C: External Faculty Witness Academic Expert Declarations, September 30 & October 1, 2025). External Faculty Witness, Plaintiff's academic mentor, has provided a declaration stating that Plaintiff's education and research have been severely derailed by his housing instability and the conflict with his former roommate.

The University's failure to accommodate Plaintiff's disability is a clear violation of Section 504 and the ADA. The statute requires that recipients of federal financial assistance, such as Duquesne University, provide reasonable accommodations to individuals with disabilities. (29 U.S.C. § 794). The University's denial of accommodation has resulted in significant harm to Plaintiff, including the loss of access to educational opportunities and emotional distress.

In addition, the University's actions demonstrate a deliberate indifference to Plaintiff's disability. (Exhibit Z-b: Deliberate Indifference Standard – Training Definition, July 28, 2020). The University's response to Plaintiff's request for accommodation was clearly unreasonable under the known circumstances, and the University failed to provide a prompt and equitable response to Plaintiff's request.

In conclusion, the University's failure to accommodate Plaintiff's disability is a clear violation of Section 504 and the ADA. The University's denial of accommodation has resulted in significant harm to Plaintiff, and the University's actions demonstrate a deliberate indifference to Plaintiff's disability. The Court should find in favor of Plaintiff on Count III.

As the Supreme Court has held, "Schoolchildren bringing ADA and Rehabilitation Act claims related to their education are not required to make a heightened showing of 'bad faith or gross misjudgment' but instead are subject to the same standards that apply in other disability discrimination contexts." (A.J.T. v. Osseo Area Schools, 605 U.S. ___ (2025)). The University's actions in this case demonstrate a clear failure to accommodate Plaintiff's disability, and the Court should hold the University liable for its failure to comply with the statute.

Furthermore, the University's actions demonstrate a pattern of deliberate indifference to Plaintiff's disability. (Exhibit X-8: Selcer Fabricates 'Performance Issues' and Threatens Stipend Through 2029, February 13, 2026). Dr. Selcer's email to Plaintiff threatening to suspend his stipend through 2029 demonstrates a clear attempt to retaliate against Plaintiff for his request for accommodation.

In conclusion, the University's failure to accommodate Plaintiff's disability is a clear violation of Section 504 and the ADA. The University's denial of accommodation has resulted in significant harm to Plaintiff, and the University's actions demonstrate a deliberate indifference to Plaintiff's disability. The Court should find in favor of Plaintiff on Count III.
