

ARGUMENT

Count I: Deliberate Indifference to Sexual Harassment under Title IX (20 U.S.C. § 1681)

Plaintiff David Mullins alleges that Defendant Duquesne University of the Holy Spirit acted with deliberate indifference to known sexual harassment, in violation of Title IX. To establish this claim, Plaintiff must demonstrate that the University had actual knowledge of the harassment, responded in a clearly unreasonable manner, and that the harassment was severe, pervasive, and objectively offensive, denying Plaintiff equal access to educational opportunities. (Davis v. Monroe County Board of Education, 526 U.S. 629, 648 (1999))

Actual Knowledge

The University had actual knowledge of the harassment through various reports and communications from Plaintiff. On June 30, 2025, Plaintiff reported the harassment to Dean Adam Wasilko, describing a physical pursuit and confrontation by his roommate, Respondent. (Exhibit B-0) Plaintiff also sent a formal Title IX escalation to Title IX Coordinator Alicia Simpson on July 24, 2025, citing 34 C.F.R. § 106.44(a) and demanding supportive measures within 48 hours. (SAC ¶¶ 168-172) Additionally, Plaintiff submitted an Emergency Removal Request to Simpson on August 13, 2025, attaching sworn declarations and a physician's letter documenting safety concerns. (Exhibit F) These reports and communications demonstrate that the University had actual knowledge of the harassment.

Clearly Unreasonable Response (Deliberate Indifference)

Despite having actual knowledge of the harassment, the University responded in a clearly unreasonable manner. The University failed to provide supportive measures, such as housing assistance, despite Plaintiff's repeated requests. (Exhibits B-0, B-5, B-5A) On August 14, 2025, Simpson reversed her jurisdictional determination, stating that the Title IX Office did not have jurisdiction over Plaintiff's report and could not take action or provide assistance. (Exhibit B-6) This response was clearly unreasonable, as it ignored the severity of the harassment and Plaintiff's safety concerns.

Severe, Pervasive, Objectively Offensive Harassment

The harassment was severe, pervasive, and objectively offensive. Respondent's behavior, including physical pursuit and confrontation, created a hostile environment that denied Plaintiff equal access to educational opportunities. (Exhibit D) Plaintiff's medical documentation confirms that he experienced stress, anxiety, and fear due to the harassment, which worsened his mental health conditions. (Exhibit F) The University's failure to respond to the harassment in a reasonable manner exacerbated the harm.

Denial of Equal Educational Access

The University's deliberate indifference to the harassment denied Plaintiff equal access to educational opportunities. Plaintiff was forced to flee his home and seek temporary housing, which disrupted his academic work and caused him significant emotional distress. (Exhibit A) The University's failure to provide supportive measures, such as housing assistance, further denied Plaintiff equal access to educational opportunities.

Conclusion

In conclusion, Plaintiff has established that the University acted with deliberate indifference to known sexual harassment, in violation of Title IX. The University had actual knowledge of the harassment, responded in a clearly unreasonable manner, and the harassment was severe, pervasive, and objectively offensive, denying Plaintiff equal access to educational opportunities. (Davis v. Monroe County Board of Education, 526 U.S. 629, 648 (1999))
