

ARGUMENT

This case presents a clear instance of deliberate indifference to known sexual harassment, in violation of Title IX. The undisputed facts demonstrate that Duquesne University, through its agents, had actual knowledge of the harassment and failed to respond in a manner that was not clearly unreasonable.

First, the University had actual knowledge of the harassment. On June 30, 2025, Plaintiff David Mullins reported the harassment to Dean Wasilko, describing a physical pursuit and confrontation by his roommate, Respondent (Exhibit B-0). This report was then forwarded to Title IX Coordinator Alicia Simpson, who acknowledged receipt of the report and offered to meet with Mullins to discuss his concerns (Exhibit B-1). As the Third Circuit has held, "actual knowledge means notice of sexual harassment or allegations of sexual harassment to... a recipient's Title IX Coordinator" (Exhibit Z-e, Slide 30).

Second, the University's response to the harassment was clearly unreasonable. Despite Mullins' repeated requests for supportive measures, including emergency housing assistance, the University failed to provide any meaningful assistance (Exhibits B-0, B-5, B-5A). In fact, the University's response was limited to requesting that Mullins meet with various administrators, without providing any clear explanation of the process or the availability of supportive measures (Exhibits B-1, B-2, B-5). As the Supreme Court has held, "a recipient's response to harassment or lack thereof is clearly unreasonable in light of the known circumstances" (Davis v. Monroe County Board of Education, 526 U.S. 629, 648 (1999)).

Third, the harassment was severe, pervasive, and objectively offensive. The Signal transcript and timeline (Exhibit D) demonstrate a pattern of harassment and intimidation by Respondent, including physical confrontations, threats, and stalking. Mullins' medical documentation (Exhibit F) also confirms that he suffered emotional distress and fear for his safety as a result of the harassment. As the Third Circuit has held, "to state a claim under Title IX, the alleged facts, if true, must support a plausible inference that a federally-funded college or university discriminated against a person on the basis of sex" (Doe v. University of the Sciences, 961 F.3d 203, 208 (3d Cir. 2020)).

Finally, the University's failure to respond to the harassment denied Mullins equal access to educational opportunities. As a result of the harassment, Mullins was forced to flee his home and seek alternative housing, which caused him significant financial and emotional distress (Exhibits B-0, G). The University's failure to provide supportive measures, such as emergency housing assistance, exacerbated this distress and denied Mullins equal access to educational opportunities (Exhibits B-5, B-5A).

In conclusion, the undisputed facts demonstrate that Duquesne University had actual knowledge of the harassment, responded in a clearly unreasonable manner, and denied Mullins equal access to educational opportunities. As such, the University is liable for deliberate indifference to known sexual harassment, in violation of Title IX.
