

ARGUMENT

Count II: Retaliation under Title IX (20 U.S.C. § 1681)

Plaintiff David Mullins brings this claim against Defendant Duquesne University of the Holy Spirit, alleging retaliation in violation of Title IX. To establish a prima facie case of retaliation, Plaintiff must show: (1) protected activity; (2) an adverse action that would deter a reasonable person; and (3) a causal connection between the protected activity and the adverse action. See Doe v. Mercy Catholic Medical Center, No. 16-1247 (3d Cir. 2017) (applying Title VII framework to Title IX retaliation claims).

Protected Activity

Plaintiff engaged in continuous protected activity from June 30, 2025, through the present, including reporting sex-based harassment to Dean Wasilko, filing a formal Title IX escalation, filing a complaint with the Office for Civil Rights (OCR), and testifying at a Temporary Restraining Order (TRO) hearing. (Exhibits B-0, B-0A, B-3A; ECF docket.) This activity is protected under Title IX, as Plaintiff was opposing and reporting sex-based harassment and discrimination. See Atkinson v. Lafayette College, 460 F.3d 447 (3d Cir. 2006) (demanding Title IX compliance is protected activity).

Adverse Action

Plaintiff suffered several adverse actions, including the removal of OCR from the CC line on emails, the denial of housing assistance, the reversal of Title IX jurisdiction, the revocation of Zoom access, and the reassignment of Plaintiff's TA position. (Exhibits B-3, B-6, Q, X-8.) These actions would deter a reasonable person from engaging in protected activity, as they undermine Plaintiff's ability to access education and participate in university life. See Jalil v. Avdel Corp., 873 F.2d 701 (3d Cir. 1989) (two-day temporal proximity between protected activity and adverse action sufficient to establish causation).

Causal Connection

The causal connection between the protected activity and the adverse action is established through temporal proximity and contextual evidence. See Lauren W. ex rel. Jean W. v. DeFlaminis, 480 F.3d 259 (3d Cir. 2007) (temporal proximity plus contextual evidence sufficient for causation in Title IX retaliation). The removal of OCR from the CC line occurred two days after Plaintiff's OCR filing, and the denial of housing assistance was made despite available resources. (Exhibits B-3, G.) The reversal of Title IX jurisdiction was made without any change in the factual record, and the revocation of Zoom access was made after Plaintiff testified at the TRO hearing. (Exhibits B-6, Q.) The reassignment of Plaintiff's TA position was made after Plaintiff reported tensions and confrontations with Faculty B, but without any evidence of performance issues. (Exhibits X-7, X-8.)

Furthermore, the pattern of antagonism and retaliation by university officials, including Dean Wasilko, Dr. Sawa, and Dr. Selcer, establishes a causal link between the protected activity and the adverse action. See Kachmar v. SunGard Data Systems, Inc., 109 F.3d 173 (3d Cir. 1997) (pattern of antagonism independently establishes causal link).

In conclusion, Plaintiff has established a prima facie case of retaliation under Title IX, demonstrating protected activity, adverse action, and a causal connection between the two. Defendant's actions, including the removal of OCR from the CC line, the denial of housing assistance, the reversal of Title IX jurisdiction, the revocation of Zoom access, and the reassignment of Plaintiff's TA position, would deter a reasonable person from engaging in protected activity and are therefore actionable under Title IX.
