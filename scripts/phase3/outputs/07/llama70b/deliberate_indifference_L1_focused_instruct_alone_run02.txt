

ARGUMENT

Count I: Deliberate Indifference to Sexual Harassment under Title IX (20 U.S.C. § 1681)

Plaintiff David Mullins brings this claim against Defendant Duquesne University of the Holy Spirit, alleging deliberate indifference to known acts of sexual harassment in violation of Title IX. The evidence demonstrates that Duquesne University had actual knowledge of the harassment, responded unreasonably, and failed to provide supportive measures, thereby denying Plaintiff equal access to educational opportunities.

Actual Knowledge

Duquesne University had actual knowledge of the harassment through various reports and communications from Plaintiff, including the initial report to Dean Wasilko on June 30, 2025 (Exhibit B-0), and the formal Title IX escalation to President Simpson on July 24, 2025 (Exhibit B-1). As the Title IX Coordinator, Simpson had authority to institute corrective measures, and her knowledge of the harassment is imputed to the University (Exhibit Z-e, Slide 30). Furthermore, the University's Responsible Employee Program, which designates all employees as mandatory reporters, ensures that every employee who receives a report has actual knowledge of the harassment (Exhibit Z-e, Slide 33).

Clearly Unreasonable Response (Deliberate Indifference)

Duquesne University's response to the harassment was clearly unreasonable. Despite Plaintiff's repeated requests for supportive measures, the University failed to provide any meaningful assistance. Simpson's initial response on August 13, 2025, acknowledged Plaintiff's concerns and offered to discuss supportive measures, but ultimately conditioned these measures on Plaintiff's participation in a meeting (Exhibit B-5). When Plaintiff refused to meet, Simpson reversed her jurisdictional determination, stating that the Title IX Office did not have jurisdiction over the report (Exhibit B-6). This reversal was not based on any new factual information, but rather on Plaintiff's protected activity under § 106.71 (Exhibits B-5A, B-5B, B-5C).

The University's failure to provide supportive measures, such as a mutual no-contact order, space/course separation, or public safety measures, was unreasonable in light of the known circumstances (Exhibit B-5A). The University's own policy requires the provision of supportive measures, which are designed to restore or preserve equal access to the education program or activity (Exhibit Z-c, Slide 38). By failing to provide these measures, the University demonstrated deliberate indifference to Plaintiff's safety and well-being.

Severe, Pervasive, Objectively Offensive Harassment

The harassment Plaintiff experienced was severe, pervasive, and objectively offensive. The Signal transcript and timeline (Exhibit D) demonstrate a pattern of behavior by Respondent that was designed to intimidate and control Plaintiff. Respondent's actions, including locking Plaintiff out of the apartment, manipulating the air conditioning, and following Plaintiff, created a hostile environment that denied Plaintiff equal access to educational opportunities.

Denial of Equal Educational Access

The University's failure to respond to the harassment and provide supportive measures denied Plaintiff equal access to educational opportunities. Plaintiff was forced to flee his home, relocate to a temporary address, and experience significant emotional distress (Exhibits F, G, R, S). The University's actions, or lack thereof, effectively excluded Plaintiff from participating in the educational program, in violation of Title IX.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the evidence demonstrates that Duquesne University had actual knowledge of the harassment, responded unreasonably, and failed to provide supportive measures, thereby denying Plaintiff equal access to educational opportunities. The University's actions constitute deliberate indifference to known acts of sexual harassment, in violation of Title IX. As held in Davis v. Monroe County Board of Education, 526 U.S. 629 (1999), a funding recipient is liable for student-on-student harassment where it acts with deliberate indifference to known harassment that is severe, pervasive, and objectively offensive enough to deny access to educational opportunities.
