**ARGUMENT**

Plaintiff David Mullins has established a prima facie case of retaliation under Title IX, which is analyzed under the Title VII burden-shifting framework. *Doe v. Mercy Catholic Medical Center*, No. 16-1247 (3d Cir. 2017). To prevail, Plaintiff must demonstrate: (1) engagement in protected activity; (2) an adverse action that would deter a reasonable person from complaining; and (3) a causal connection between the two. *Doe*, No. 16-1247.

**I. Plaintiff Engaged in Continuous Protected Activity**

Under Title IX, "demanding compliance with Title IX is protected activity." *Atkinson v. Lafayette College*, 460 F.3d 447 (3d Cir. 2006). Furthermore, a plaintiff need only hold an "objectively reasonable belief, in good faith" that the conduct they oppose violates the statute. *Kengerski v. Harper*, No. 20-1307 (3d Cir. 2021). 

Plaintiff’s record demonstrates a continuous stream of protected activity from June 2025 through the present:
*   **Reporting Sex-Based Harassment:** On June 30, 2025, Plaintiff reported stalking and intimidation by a female roommate to Dean Wasilko, explicitly seeking "emergency housing options" and "protective campus measures." (Ex. B-0).
*   **Opposing Gender Bias:** On July 23, 2025, Plaintiff challenged the University’s minimization of his safety concerns, questioning whether the response "would have been the same had the genders in this situation been reversed." (Ex. B-0A).
*   **Demanding Statutory Compliance:** On July 24, 2025, Plaintiff formally escalated his concerns, citing 34 C.F.R. § 106.44(a) regarding the University's duty to respond promptly and not be deliberately indifferent. (Ex. B-1).
*   **External Agency Escalation:** On July 26, 2025, Plaintiff referred his case to the U.S. Department of Education Office for Civil Rights (OCR), citing "Title IX Retaliation" and "ongoing failure to provide Title IX-mandated protection." (Ex. B-3A).
*   **Judicial Participation:** Plaintiff further engaged in protected activity by filing a federal lawsuit (Sept. 9), filing a TRO motion (Oct. 8), and testifying under oath at the TRO hearing (Oct. 15). (ECF docket; TRO Tr.).

**II. Defendant Subjected Plaintiff to Multiple Adverse Actions**

An adverse action is one that would deter a reasonable person from engaging in protected activity. The record reveals a pattern of institutional actions designed to isolate and penalize Plaintiff:

*   **Threats of Disciplinary Action:** On August 15, 2025, Dr. Anne Sawa threatened Plaintiff with "charges against you for violating the Student Code of Conduct" specifically because he continued to send emails to University officials regarding his safety and access. (Ex. B-7).
*   **Academic Coercion:** Dean Blair threatened Plaintiff with "academic failure" if he did not attend Dr. Selcer’s class in person, despite the active federal litigation between Plaintiff and Selcer. (SAC ¶¶ 66, 76, 336).
*   **Revocation of Educational Access:** On October 17, 2025, Dr. Selcer revoked Plaintiff’s Zoom access—an arrangement previously authorized by Dr. Faculty C (Ex. W) and affirmed by Selcer under oath at the TRO hearing (TRO Tr.).
*   **Financial and Professional Penalties:** On February 13, 2026, Dr. Selcer reassigned Plaintiff’s TA position and issued a written threat that "future significant performance issues" could result in the "suspension of your stipend" through Spring 2029. (Ex. X-8).

**III. A Causal Connection Exists Between the Protected Activity and the Adverse Actions**

Causation may be established through "unusually suggestive" temporal proximity or a "convincing mosaic of circumstantial evidence." *Canada v. Samuel Grossi & Sons, Inc.*, No. 20-2747 (3d Cir. 2022); *Kachmar v. SunGard Data Systems, Inc.*, 109 F.3d 173 (3d Cir. 1997).

**A. Suggestive Temporal Proximity**
The Third Circuit has held that a "two-day temporal proximity between protected activity and adverse action is sufficient to establish causation." *Jalil v. Avdel Corp.*, 873 F. 2d 701 (3d Cir. 1989). Here, the timing is precisely suggestive:
*   **OCR Filing $\rightarrow$ Institutional Erasure:** Plaintiff filed his OCR complaint on July 26; two days later, on July 28, Dean Wasilko removed OCR from the CC line of the responsive email. (Ex. B-3, B-3A).
*   **TRO Testimony $\rightarrow$ Access Revocation:** Plaintiff testified at the TRO hearing on October 15; two days later, on October 17, Dr. Selcer revoked his Zoom access. (TRO Tr.; Ex. Q).
*   **Jurisdictional Flip:** On August 13, 2025, at 2:02 PM, Alicia Simpson acknowledged Title IX jurisdiction and "supportive measures." (Ex. B-5). After Plaintiff sent three emails consisting entirely of protected activity and OCR restoration (Ex. B-5A, B-5B, B-5C), Simpson reversed her jurisdictional determination on August 14 at 9:42 AM—a gap of only nineteen hours. (Ex. B-6).

**B. The "Convincing Mosaic" of Pretext and Antagonism**
Beyond timing, the "proffered evidence, looked at as a whole," establishes a retaliatory motive. *Farrell v. Planters Lifesavers Co.*, 206 F.3d 271 (3d Cir. 2000). 

First, the University engaged in a systematic effort to shield its conduct from federal oversight. Between July 28 and October 2025, four institutional actors removed OCR from the CC line on eight or more occasions, each time immediately after Plaintiff restored them. (Ex. B-3 through B-7).

Second, the University utilized fabricated justifications to penalize Plaintiff. On February 13, 2026, Dr. Selcer claimed he was reassigning Plaintiff due to "performance issues raised by two of the three faculty members." (Ex. X-8). However, this is contradicted by:
1.  **Direct Evidence:** Faculty B’s same-day email explicitly stated there was "no performance complaint" and that her "door is always open." (Ex. X-7).
2.  **Prior Testimony:** Dr. Selcer testified at the TRO hearing that Plaintiff had no performance issues. (TRO Tr.).
3.  **Contradictory Records:** In a meeting on February 10, Selcer used no "performance issues" language and confirmed that Faculty B "doesn't make decisions about your stipend." (Ex. X-6).

Under *Canada*, an employer's motivation for investigating or disciplining a plaintiff is itself evidence of pretext. The fabrication of "performance issues" to justify a stipend threat—occurring in the wake of a federal lawsuit—completes the mosaic of retaliation.

**Conclusion**
Because Plaintiff engaged in protected activity, suffered adverse actions that would deter any reasonable student, and demonstrated a causal link through both suggestive temporal proximity and a pattern of fabricated justifications, he has stated a claim for retaliation under Title IX.
