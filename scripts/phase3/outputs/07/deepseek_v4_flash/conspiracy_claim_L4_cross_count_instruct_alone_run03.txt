**ARGUMENT**

Plaintiff David Mullins has adduced more than sufficient evidence to create genuine disputes of material fact on each element of his civil conspiracy claims under Pennsylvania common law and 42 U.S.C. § 1985. Each of Defendants' arguments fails because it asks this Court to resolve factual disputes against Plaintiff, to ignore direct evidence of coordination, or to disregard the governing legal framework—all improper at summary judgment.

**I. Defendants' Argument That Blair's Consultation with Selcer Was "Routine Academic Administration" Fails Because TAP 33 Required Independent Review, and Their Coordination Constitutes Direct Evidence of Unlawful Agreement.**

Defendants contend that Dean Blair's consultation with Dr. Selcer was "routine academic administration" because she "could not respond to Plaintiff's transfer request without checking course availability and academic progress with the department chair." Dkt. 34 at 10. This characterization is contradicted by the documentary record and by the plain terms of the university's own conflict-of-interest policy.

On October 20, 2025, Plaintiff submitted a conflict-of-interest disclosure to Dean Blair, specifically advising her that Dr. Selcer—Plaintiff's instructor in the required teaching seminar—was "a named defendant in my ongoing federal civil case against Duquesne University." Exhibit K-1. Plaintiff requested "an alternate instructor or equivalent course" and stated that he would "refrain from attending to avoid any appearance of impropriety or further conflict." *Id.*

TAP 33 § IV.4 requires *independent* review of conflict-of-interest disclosures. Exhibit K-2; TAP 33 § IV.4. Yet instead of conducting an independent review, Dean Blair wrote on October 22, 2025: "I did need to consult Dr. Selcer as instructor to secure some additional details. Both Dr. Selcer and I strongly concur that it is appropriate for you to remain enrolled in your required teaching seminar." Exhibit K-2 (emphasis added). This is a written admission of joint determination with the conflicted party—"both of us, together"—rather than the independent review the policy mandates. *Id.*

Under Pennsylvania law, a civil conspiracy requires "two or more persons combined or agreed with intent to do an unlawful act or to do an otherwise lawful act by unlawful means." *Thompson Coal Co. v. Pike Coal Co.,* 488 Pa. 198, 412 A.2d 466, 472-73 (1979). Dean Blair's email is direct evidence of an agreement: she concedes she consulted the conflicted party, and she announces their "strong concur[rence]." Exhibit K-2. That Selcer was the subject of the conflict-of-interest disclosure makes his participation in the decision all the more significant. Any "routine" administration would have required Blair to review the matter independently; instead, she teamed with Selcer to reach a predetermined outcome.

The timing further underscores the retaliatory nature of this agreement. Blair consulted Selcer just seven days after Plaintiff cross-examined Selcer under oath at the TRO hearing on October 15, 2025. Exhibit K-2; TRO Tr. As *Jalil v. Avdel Corp.* holds, "[t]wo-day temporal proximity between protected activity and adverse action is sufficient to establish causation." 873 F.2d 701, 708 (3d Cir. 1989). Seven days is "unusually suggestive" under *Jalil*. The inference of retaliatory agreement is overwhelming.

Defendants' characterization of this consultation as "routine" is a factual characterization that must be resolved at trial, not on summary judgment. *See Thompson Coal,* 412 A.2d at 472-73 (conspiracy may be proven by circumstantial evidence). There is a genuine dispute whether Blair's consultation was a routine administrative step or a coordinated effort to maintain Selcer's control over Plaintiff despite a clear conflict of interest.

**II. Defendants' Argument That the Consultation Evidenced "Intent to Help, Not Malice" Is Contradicted by the Sequence of Events and the Policy's Plain Terms.**

Defendants argue that the consultation "resulted in assurances of fair treatment and offers of academic support." Dkt. 34 at 10. Even if true, the subjective intent of the conspirators is not an element of civil conspiracy under Pennsylvania law. *Thompson Coal* requires "proof of malice, i.e., an intent to injure," 412 A.2d at 472-73, but malice may be inferred from the nature of the agreement and the acts taken in furtherance of it. *Accord Hutchison v. Luddy,* 870 A.2d 766, 772 (Pa. 2005) (punitive damages may be awarded where "a defendant had a subjective appreciation of the risk of harm to which the plaintiff was exposed and that he acted ... in conscious disregard of that risk").

The evidence demonstrates that the "assurances" and "support" were illusory. On February 13, 2026—just four months after Blair's "assurance" that Selcer would provide "the same consideration and opportunities as any other student," Exhibit K-2—Selcer fabricated "performance issues" and threatened to suspend Plaintiff's stipend through 2029. Exhibit X-8. Selcer's email referenced "a recommendation by the Chair to the Dean of Liberal Arts"—Blair's exact title—demonstrating ongoing communication between them. *Id.;* see also Exhibit X-6 (February 10, 2026 meeting transcript: no "performance issues" language appears anywhere). The "recommendation to the Dean" language did not appear in the February 10 meeting, proving intervening communication between Selcer and Blair. Compare Exhibit X-6 with Exhibit X-8.

If the conspiracy's objective was to help Plaintiff, it is inexplicable why Selcer would fabricate "performance issues," why he would threaten stipend suspension through 2029, and why he would reference a "recommendation to the Dean" that—on the record—had no basis in fact. The only reasonable inference is malice: an intent to injure Plaintiff for exercising his federally protected rights. *See Canada v. Samuel Grossi & Sons, Inc.,* No. 20-2747 (3d Cir. 2022) ("The employer's motivation for investigating the plaintiff is itself evidence of pretext").

**III. Defendants' Argument That No Underlying Tort Exists Because Blair's TAP 33 Analysis Was "Correct" Is Itself a Factual Dispute That Cannot Be Resolved on Summary Judgment, and Plaintiff Has Adduced Evidence of Multiple Underlying Torts.**

Defendants argue that "no underlying tort exists to support the conspiracy because Blair's TAP 33 analysis was correct on the policy's plain terms." Dkt. 34 at 10. This argument fails for three independent reasons.

*First,* whether TAP 33's definition of "Covered Party" or "Conflict of Interest" excludes Plaintiff's situation is a question of contract interpretation that is genuinely disputed. Plaintiff's Counter-Statement of Material Facts ¶ 4 establishes that "TAP 33 § IV.4 requires independent review of conflict-of-interest disclosures" and that "Blair's 'consult[ation]' with the conflicted party does not constitute independent review under any reading of the policy." *Id.;* see also Exhibit K-2. The policy's language—"independent review"—creates a genuine dispute as to whether consulting the very person whose conflict was disclosed can satisfy that standard. Defendants cannot obtain summary judgment by asserting their own interpretation of a disputed policy provision.

*Second,* even if TAP 33 did not apply, Plaintiff has adduced evidence of multiple underlying torts committed by co-conspirators, for which Blair is jointly and severally liable under *Thompson Coal*, 412 A.2d at 472-73 ("once a defendant joins a conspiracy, that defendant is jointly and severally liable for all tortious acts committed by any co-conspirator in furtherance of the conspiracy"). The record demonstrates:

- **Fraud:** Selcer fabricated "performance issues" that were "contradicted by the source he cited on the same day." Counter-Statement ¶ 9; compare Exhibit X-7 (Faculty B: "My office door is always open to you"; no performance complaint) with Exhibit X-8 (Selcer: "performance issues raised by two of the three faculty members"). *Gibbs v. Ernst,* 538 Pa. 193, 647 A.2d 882, 889 (1994) (fraud requires "falsely, with knowledge of its falsity" representation intended to induce reliance).

- **Fraudulent Concealment:** Blair and Selcer systematically removed OCR and senior administrative oversight from correspondence at critical junctures, preventing Plaintiff from receiving corrective information. Counter-Statement ¶ 7; Exhibits B-3 through B-7; Selcer Sept 3 email. *Moser v. DeSetta,* 589 A.2d 679, 682 (Pa. Super. 1991) ("[t]he concealment of a material fact can amount to a culpable misrepresentation no less than does an intentional false statement").

- **Deliberate Indifference:** Defendants' coordinated refusal to provide supportive measures despite actual knowledge of severe harassment constitutes deliberate indifference under *Davis v. Monroe County Board of Education,* 526 U.S. 629, 648 (1999). Plaintiff's medical documentation confirms "homelessness since June 30th which has been very stressful and likely worsens his mental health conditions." Exhibit F. Dean Wasilko expressly denied housing despite available resources. Exhibit G. *See Hall v. Millersville University,* No. 19-3275 (3d Cir. Jan. 11, 2022) (failure to follow Title IX policy constitutes clearly unreasonable response).

*Third,* Defendants' assertion that the analysis was "correct" is a factual claim that must be tested at trial. It does not entitle them to judgment as a matter of law because "[t]he FCA's materiality requirement is satisfied when the false statement has a natural tendency to influence, or is capable of influencing, the payment or receipt of money or property." *United States ex rel. Moore & Co. v. Majestic Blue Fisheries, LLC,* 812 F.3d 294, 300 (3d Cir. 2016) (by analogy, requiring that false statements be material to the government's payment decision). The same principle applies here: whether TAP 33 applied is relevant to whether Defendants' actions were lawful or unlawful, not whether summary judgment is appropriate.

**IV. Defendants' Argument That Plaintiff's Characterization Reflects "Refusal to Engage" Rather Than "Unlawful Agreement" Ignores the Coordinated Pattern of Conduct Established in the Record.**

Defendants argue that "Plaintiff's characterization of coordinated institutional responses as 'conspiracy' reflects his refusal to engage with well-established university processes, not evidence of unlawful agreement." Dkt. 25 at 6. This argument mischaracterizes the record and ignores settled law.

The evidence demonstrates a pattern of coordination, not independent decision-making:

- **August 13-14, 2025:** Simpson initially accepted Plaintiff's Title IX complaint, then explicitly disclaimed jurisdiction. Exhibits B-5, B-6. This 24-hour flip is circumstantial evidence of an agreement to deprive Plaintiff of Title IX protections.

- **August 15, 2025:** Sawa threatened disciplinary charges for seeking help from university officials. Exhibit B-7 ("you must stop sending mass emails to University officials ... Failure to do so may result in charges against you for violating the Student Code of Conduct"). *See Hall,* slip op. at 12 (threatening a complainant with discipline for reporting harassment is itself retaliatory).

- **September 3, 2025:** Selcer removed Blair herself from the "final directive," demonstrating that even the conspirators recognized the impropriety of their coordination. Counter-Statement ¶ 7; Selcer Sept 3 email.

- **October 17, 2025:** Selcer contacted Faculty C to revoke Zoom access that Faculty C had already authorized. Exhibits Q, V. Selcer presented Faculty C's own pre-existing authorization as if Faculty C were independently evaluating a "compelling reason" standard that Faculty C never knew he was applying. *Id.*

- **February 13, 2026:** Selcer's reassignment email referenced a "recommendation to the Dean of Liberal Arts"—Blair's exact title—proving communication between them in the days preceding the fabricated "performance issues" threat. Exhibit X-8; Counter-Statement ¶ 5.

This is not a student who "refused to engage." This is a student who engaged repeatedly and was met with coordination, obstruction, retaliation, and deliberate indifference. As *Canada v. Samuel Grossi & Sons, Inc.* holds, "a convincing mosaic of circumstantial evidence is sufficient to establish causation for retaliation claims." No. 20-2747 (3d Cir. 2022). That "mosaic" is present here.

**V. Defendants' Argument That Selcer's Continued Role Was Not Retaliatory Because He Held That Role "Before and After" Protected Activity Is Legally Irrelevant; Continued Adverse Action Is an Overt Act in Furtherance of the Conspiracy.**

Defendants argue that "Selcer's continuation as Plaintiff's instructor was not retaliatory because he held that role before and after Plaintiff's protected activity—therefore not an overt act in furtherance of conspiracy." Dkt. 34 at 10 n.3. This argument misapplies the law of conspiracy and retaliation.

*First,* under *Thompson Coal,* "[o]nce a defendant joins a conspiracy, that defendant is jointly and severally liable for all tortious acts committed by any co-conspirator in furtherance of the conspiracy." 412 A.2d at 472-73. Selcer's continuation as Plaintiff's instructor was not a static fact—it was an active decision by the conspirators to maintain Selcer's control over Plaintiff's academic progress despite Plaintiff's conflict-of-interest disclosure. Blair's October 22, 2025 email confirms that continuation was a *joint determination*, not a default. Exhibit K-2 ("Both Dr. Selcer and I strongly concur that it is appropriate for you to remain enrolled").

*Second,* in the retaliation context, a "continuing adverse action" may constitute retaliation when it is imposed or maintained in response to protected activity. *Kachmar v. SunGard Data Systems, Inc.,* 109 F.3d 173, 178 (3d Cir. 1997) ("it is causation, not temporal proximity itself, that is an element of plaintiff's prima facie case"). The "pattern of antagonism" following protected conduct—including Selcer's retaliation for Plaintiff's Title IX demands, the removal of Zoom access, the fabrication of "performance issues," and the threat to suspend Plaintiff's stipend through 2029—demonstrates that Selcer's role was not benign but was a tool of the conspiracy. *Id.* at 177.
